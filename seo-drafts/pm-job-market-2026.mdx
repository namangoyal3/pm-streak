There are more than 7,300 open PM roles at tech companies globally right now—a 75% recovery from the brutal 2023 lows, and up nearly 20% since January of this year. On paper, the market is back.

But if you've been applying for more than 30 days without callbacks, you're experiencing the other half of the story: this recovery is brutally selective. The headline numbers are being held up by a small segment of in-demand profiles. Everyone else is competing for a thin remainder.

Here's what's actually happening—and how to put yourself on the right side of it.

## The Recovery Is Real, But It's AI-Gated

The single biggest driver of the current hiring surge is AI. AI-related PM job postings increased 340% since 2024. Traditional PM roles—owning a feature area, running sprints, writing PRDs for non-AI surfaces—are flat or declining.

Atlassian made this dynamic explicit: in 2025 they cut 1,600 generalist positions while simultaneously opening 800 AI-focused roles. That's not a reduction in headcount—it's a composition shift. And it's happening across the industry.

What this means in practice: if your resume centers on classic PM execution—OKRs, sprint ceremonies, stakeholder alignment, roadmap delivery—you're competing for a shrinking pool of roles against an increasing number of candidates who look exactly like you.

### Which Companies Are Hiring vs. Contracting

The composition shift is uneven across company stages:

**Hiring aggressively:** AI-native startups (Series A–C), hyperscaler AI product divisions (Google DeepMind PM teams, Meta GenAI, OpenAI product), and enterprise software companies with active AI transformation programs.

**Flat or contracting:** Legacy enterprise software companies without a credible AI story, e-commerce PMs without data/personalization background, and pre-product-market-fit startups where the PM role hasn't been defined yet.

## The Profile That Gets Callbacks Right Now

Hiring managers in 2026 are pattern-matching for one of two profiles:

**Profile A: Senior PM with demonstrated AI impact.** This doesn't mean "used ChatGPT for notes." It means: led a team that shipped an AI-powered feature, can speak fluently about model evaluation and production tradeoffs, and has the metrics to prove the feature worked. Bonus points: you've navigated a responsible AI conversation with legal or customers.

**Profile B: PM with deep technical fluency.** Python and SQL depth. Comfortable pulling your own data. Has done prompt engineering in a product context. Can hold a real conversation with an ML engineer without a translator. Has a track record of shipping fast in ambiguous environments.

If you don't fit either profile, that's not a career death sentence—but it's a positioning problem you need to solve before your next application.

## Salary vs. Volume: The Paradox Explained

Here's the counterintuitive data: PM hiring was down approximately 14% in 2025, but median PM salaries rose approximately 5% over the same period. How?

Supply and demand at the quality tier. Fewer PM jobs were being filled, but the ones being filled went to highly qualified candidates who commanded premium compensation. The bottom of the market compressed or disappeared entirely.

This trend is continuing into 2026. Senior PMs with AI impact stories and demonstrated cross-functional leadership are seeing compensation packages 20–35% above market comps from two years ago. Mid-market PMs who've been grinding at similar roles for 3–5 years without a clear differentiation story are flat or being displaced by candidates willing to take a title step-down for the right role.

### U.S. Salary Ranges by Level (2026)

- **APM / Associate PM:** $110–135K base
- **PM:** $140–180K base
- **Senior PM:** $180–240K base
- **Staff / Principal PM:** $240–310K base
- **Group PM / Director:** $280–380K base + equity

AI-adjacent roles at these levels are running 15–25% above these numbers. Remote roles are running 10–18% below Bay Area comps.

## What Actually Gets Resumes Past ATS in 2026

Three resume changes that consistently move the needle:

**1. Quantify AI feature impact, not AI tool use.** Don't write: "Used AI to improve team productivity." Write: "Led AI-powered search feature that increased successful query rate from 34% to 67%, reducing support tickets 22% in the first 90 days post-launch."

**2. Own your metrics section.** Hiring managers at AI-first companies care about what you measured, not just what you shipped. For each role, include: the metric you owned, the baseline, the result, and the timeframe. Four data points per role, every role.

**3. Signal technical depth without overclaiming.** Most PMs either bury their technical background or overclaim coding ability they don't have. The sweet spot: "Writes SQL for analysis, reviews engineering designs, shipped 3 LLM-integrated features." Specific. Honest. Differentiating.

## The Geography Premium Isn't Gone

Remote's dominance in PM hiring peaked in 2023. Today, the Bay Area in-office or hybrid premium is real. Senior AI-adjacent PM roles in San Francisco are paying 18–25% more than comparable remote roles, and for the most strategic positions, companies are explicitly requiring Bay Area presence.

If you're remote-only and can't or won't relocate, the market still exists—but your job is to make your remote productivity story undeniable. Build your async communication habits, your documentation standards, and your ability to drive decisions across time zones. These are exactly what hiring managers probe in remote PM interviews.

## The Junior PM Problem: An Honest Assessment

Junior PMs face the sharpest competition in this market: fewer entry-level openings, more applicants, and a hiring bar that has moved up substantially.

The companies that still hire APMs and junior PMs are doing so because they believe in the pipeline—they want to develop talent they can grow. What they're looking for has shifted: strong analytical skills (SQL matters even at this level), evidence of shipping something (personal project, student PM role, anything with a user and a metric), and genuine AI curiosity demonstrated through real experience, not just listed as a skill.

The fastest path to a junior PM offer right now: build something with AI that solves a real problem, document the problem → solution → metrics loop publicly, and use it as your opening story in every interview.

## How to Calibrate Your Job Search

The market moves fast for the right profile and goes silent for everyone else. If you're getting fewer than 2 interview invitations per 20 applications, that's a positioning problem—not a pipeline problem. Applying more won't fix it.

The diagnostic question: what's your strongest AI-adjacent story? If you don't have one from your current role, build one on the side. Take a public problem, apply an AI solution, measure the outcome, document it publicly. A well-framed portfolio project consistently outperforms a vague work history in AI PM interviews right now.

If you're already in a strong position, the highest-leverage move is being ready before you need to search. Keep your interview skills sharp, stay current on AI product concepts, and have a story ready that answers the question every AI PM hiring manager is asking: "Have you actually shipped something with AI, and did it work?"

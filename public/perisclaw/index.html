How it works

Three steps between you and a reply.

1

Find

Every morning, Perisclaw scans new postings across the major job boards and scores each one against your profile. Weak fits are dropped before you ever see them.

2

Verify

For each role, it identifies the hiring manager or recruiter and verifies that their work email is deliverable. No verified address, no send — guessed emails bounce, and bounces hurt you.

3

Reach out

A short note written for that specific role goes out from your address with your resume attached, spaced across the morning hours when replies are most likely. Every send is logged in your activity feed.

What actually gets sent

One email, written for one person.

Three sentences about them, one about you, one ask. Hiring managers reply to notes that respect their time.

FAQ

Fair questions.

Is this the same as auto-applying to jobs?

No. Auto-apply tools submit forms into applicant tracking systems, where most applications are never read by a human. Perisclaw emails the person doing the hiring directly — that's where interviews come from.

Will automated outreach hurt my email reputation?

The pipeline only sends to verified addresses, enforces conservative daily caps, and spaces sends at a human pace. Contacts that can't be verified are dropped, not guessed.

Do the emails come from my address?

Yes. Outreach goes out from your own email account, in your own voice. Replies come straight back to you, and you can take over any thread at any time.

Where do the contacts come from?

Public sources: company team pages, announcements, and standard email patterns — verified before use. No scraped private databases, no purchased lists.
